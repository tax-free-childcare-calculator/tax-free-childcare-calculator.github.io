Eligibility Criteria for Tax-Free Childcare

Eligibility Criteria

Parents or Guardians

  • You must earn at least £152 per week (on average). This is equivalent to 16 hours per week at the National Minimum or Living Wage.
  • If you are in a couple, both partners must meet the minimum earnings threshold unless one of you is unable to work due to reasons such as disability or parental leave.
  • Each partner must not earn more than £100,000 per year.

Eligible Children

  • The child must be under 12 years old. If the child has a disability, they must be under 17 years old.
  • The childcare provider must be registered with the scheme and approved by the government.

Exceptions and Special Cases

There are exceptions for certain situations:

  • If one parent is unable to work due to disability or severe health conditions, they may still qualify.
  • Families on certain benefits, such as Universal Credit or Jobseeker’s Allowance, are not eligible for Tax-Free Childcare but may receive other childcare support.

How to Check Eligibility

You can check your eligibility and apply for Tax-Free Childcare through the official GOV.UK website. The application process is straightforward and includes identity verification and proof of income.

Why is Meeting Eligibility Important?

Ensuring you meet the eligibility criteria is essential to receive the maximum benefits. Tax-Free Childcare can significantly reduce your childcare expenses, allowing you to focus on work or other priorities without financial strain.
